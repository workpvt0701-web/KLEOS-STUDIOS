Key Art · Moksha

THE TOWER

The Brief

Before a single floor could be designed, Moksha needed a face — one image that said everything the game is without explaining any of it. The brief was simple and merciless: make someone feel the climb before they know a thing about the rules. One look, and they should understand that this is a place you ascend, that it is vast, and that it does not promise you'll make it out.

The Process

The tower became the answer — a gothic spire clawing up through a sky of fire and storm, chained to the earth it rises from, lit by a single red glow at its peak. That light is the summit: the way out, the thing every floor is climbed toward. The hundred floors aren't drawn literally; they're felt, in a structure too immense to take in at once — exactly how it should feel to stand at the base looking up. The palette stays disciplined: deep blacks, ember reds, a muted gold for the title, so the image reads as cinematic rather than loud.

The Result

The opening image of the entire series, and the bar everything after it has to clear. It set the promise in three lines — 100 floors, one escape, no guarantees — and gave Moksha a north star to be drawn toward, one piece at a time.
